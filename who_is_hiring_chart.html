HN: Who is hiring? comments per month

Data fetched from HN Algolia API on 2026-06-21 10:36:17 (UTC).

Methodology & definitions

WIH comments are the comment counts on the monthly “Ask HN: Who is hiring?” post.

HN comments are all comments across Hacker News in that month (any post), estimated via the HN Algolia Search API.

Normalize means: WIH comments ÷ total HN comments, shown as “comments per 10k HN comments” to make month‑to‑month comparisons fairer.

Categories are assigned by keyword matching on each job comment. Each comment is assigned to the first matching bucket, otherwise “Other.”

Crypto/Web3 and Cryptography are separate buckets to avoid mixing cryptocurrencies with security/crypto research.

  • We select one “Who is hiring?” post per month (highest comment count if duplicates).
  • We drop the first and last months to avoid partial months.
  • Monthly HN comment totals are computed by summing 2‑day buckets, and falling back to 1‑day buckets when Algolia marks a result as non‑exhaustive.
  • The smoothing line is a 12‑month moving average over the selected series (raw or normalized).
  • The category chart is a 100% stacked area (each month sums to 100%).
